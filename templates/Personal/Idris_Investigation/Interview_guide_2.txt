Field Guide
[/center][hr]Subject: Interrogations and Interviews, part 2
A proper interview is vital to resolving a matter involving personnel, and discretion is paramount to this. Do not accuse without evidence, instead attempt to extract facts without causing any scandals. Establishing who is at fault for the incident and a timeline of what occured. Above all else, remember: Be polite, be neutral, be in charge.
[hr][h3]General questioning of involved parties[/h3][b]Objective:[/b] Confirm facts, align statements with evidence.
Establish what occured, noting contradictions indicating guilt.
[hr]Recommended approach:
[list][*]Approach the involved party and keep a firm but polite tone.[list][*]"I am investigator (name), representing Idris."
[*]"For our records, I need your direct account for what happened."
[*]"Your cooperation ensures this matter will be swiftly resolved."
[/list][*]Start with open ended questions to establish an oversight.[list][*]"Where were you when the event occured?"
[*]"What did you see?"
[*]"Can you describe what you heard?"
[/list][*]Move to specific clarifications.[list][*]"At what exact time did this occur?"
[*]"Could you clarify (detail)?"
[*]Did you notice anyone else in the area?"
[/list][*]Verify accounts and evidence.[list][*]"You stated you were at (location). Can you confirm the time you left?"
[*]"Our records show you near (location). Can you explain this?"
[*]"According to our evidence, your (prints/fibers) were found at (location/object)."
[/list][*]Closing:[list][*]"This concludes my questions. If anything else occurs to you, please contact me. Here are my details."
[*]"Your cooperation will be noted, thank you for your time."
[*]"Thank you for your assistance. Idris appreciates your time."
[*]"This concludes my questions. Please wait here, an officer will be by shortly."
